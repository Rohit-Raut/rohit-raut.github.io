Research Focus

My work spans across experimental particle physics, focusing on neutrino detection, dark matter search, and detector development for the DUNE experiment.

Neutrino Detection

Working on high voltage systems and photon detection for the DUNE experiment. Focused on low energy calibration using Bismuth-207 sources and developing purity monitoring systems. Contributing to field cage construction which provides sustained electric field inside the cryostat for optimal particle detection.

Dark Matter Search

Analyzing ICARUS data for beyond Standard Model physics with focus on boosted dark matter interactions. Contributing to the DAMSA experiment, investigating low-energy nuclear recoil signatures in liquid argon detectors and developing novel search strategies for inelastic dark matter scattering events.

Detector Development

Optimizing LArTPC systems at CERN and Fermilab with emphasis on readout electronics and triggering algorithms. Contributing to cryogenic testing and calibration procedures. Conducting feasibility studies for the DAMSA experiment and performing quantum efficiency and lifetime measurements for Large Area Picosecond Photodetectors (LAPPDs).
